Smart Home For Dummies

Understanding the basics of home automation


Today in the ever changing world of technology, the market for smart homes and automation technology products is getting bigger. It's no longer a futuristic cartoon where the home is fully automatic, it’s actually a reality.
On the market there is a huge range of smart home devices and gadgets, starting from lighting, to audio & video equipment, home security systems, thermostats, irrigation, blinds, power points, garage doors, etc. Building a living space that’s automated is easy – as it doesn’t require any scientific knowledge or an IT engineering background to do so.

Smart home automation is for everyone!

All you need now is to know where to start. Here at House-Mate Automation smart technologies and home automation devices are what we’re all about. If you’re just starting in this space the best thing to do is start with the fundamentals. When talking about smart homes or home automation in general, we’re referring to a variety of devices, gadgets, appliances, and systems, which are all connected and communicating with each other, in order to manage your home. Once connected they can act autonomously without any input to operate your home - all according to your custom requirements.

There are a wide range of devices that fall into this 'smart' category. Ranging from devices that aren’t fully automated on their their own, to devices that have semi-automatic operation, to devices that have a one-time setup and then can be forgotten. Many devices and gadgets require minimal input from the user, which is usually controlled via a smartphone, tablet, or even via voice or gesture recognition. For example: door locks that unlock or lock via presence as they know whether you are home or away, or lights that you can turn on or off through voice, or operate remotely when you’re not there at all. Either way the goal is to reduce or even completely remove the human input to get things done around your home.

Another thing to understand when you’re looking to automating your home is that the type of device can vary. As an example, smart lights. There are smart lighting systems, smart light switches, or even single light bulbs.
Taking it a step further, smart lights can be programmed to turn on or off independently. It could be at certain times they are scheduled to operate, or they can be triggered by other devices such as motion sensors which can switch lights on automatically when entering a room. Most semi-automatic devices can also be connected to work within a fully automated home system, removing the need for human intervention completely.

Understanding the concepts of Home Automation

Think of the smart home as a machine consisting of many parts with everything connected and communicating within the system once properly setup and programmed. Lighting, heating, security, and entertainment devices all have their own role to play but everything is essentially working together to deliver an outcome. For example: The system can detect whats happening and trigger devices to work together. When the last person leaves the house, turn off the heating, switch off the lights and set the alarm system to 'away' mode. This example is where different devices are working together and responding to the commands or instructions that you can program.

The common thing between most of the smart devices is having “sensors”, they can sense certain changes or things happening around the house. The sensors here are the eyes and ears of your automated home, it’s the automated source of input for your smart home. The next step they perform is responding by taking action and passing the information to your other connected devices. As an example, light system could be set to turn off automatically according to the thermostat which knows you’re not home.

When a certain sensor is active, the smart devices is already programmed and knows it’s job, smoothly actioning everything you want done. Another example is Apple HomeKit or Amazon's Alexa taking your voice command to make coffee or if there’s a connected motion sensor, it would know that when you wake up, and automatically pass this information to your smart coffee machine, which will automatically work and make your morning coffee with zero effort from your side. Using those smart devices and building a fully automated home system will change your daily home routine, with a good part of it happening automatically. Making life simple!

What exactly can I automate?

The massive tech advancements over the last few years have made it possible to automate almost everything in your home. Previously the home automation systems were simple... limited to timer lamps and some automatic recording features for TVs and VCRs. Things have evolved considerably. Nowadays, automation tech and smart home devices are on every manufacturers roadmap. So many house devices and gadgets can be automated that it's now possible to literally program your home, forget about it, and let it run on its own! Thermostats that know exactly the temperature you prefer, irrigation that operates according to local weather forecasts, blinds that can go up or down according to temperature are just a few more examples. These advancements have changed the way people interact with their homes, alot like mobile banking has changed the way people interact with their bank.

What to automate first? It’s your decision now!

Take a look at your daily routine. The things you need to do on daily basis, the boring home maintenance tasks and how often you have to do each. There are many daily based tasks you do without even noticing or paying attention and too many hours pass while you’re doing the same tasks every day. As an example, do you walk throughout the home each night and switch off multiple lights before going to bed? Or do you forget to turn the thermostat down and leave the heating on accidentally?

Asking yourself these questions can help identify the most repetitive tasks that you carry out. They are the perfect starting point to start building your dream smart home. Once you have identified the boring tasks, let’s automate that, and House-Mate can help you find the best smart device for that job.
Now that you know the concepts behind home automation, have a good think about all the what’s and how’s, let’s step forward together and build your smart home.
