Smart Locks

The advantages and disadvantages of smart locks

Keyless entry, often facilitated by smart lock technology, has revolutionized the way we secure and access our homes and properties. This advanced system eliminates the need for traditional keys, offering a more convenient and secure approach to locking and unlocking doors.

The most popular and simplest to install is one with a battery as a power source. Usually, where a 12V battery tube is used it is supplied with rechargeable lithium batteries. However a more durable solution is connection to mains power but that comes with additional cost as an electrician is required to wire the 240volt connection to the locks. A continuous power source can be hard-wired or the plug-in type. The plug-in power will need an outlet for your power source, which is an advantage if you already have your windows close to an outlet. The plug-in type of motorized keyless-entrys is best for larger types of windows that may drain batteries with frequent use. If you want to cut costs on your electricity usage, a solar energy source is also available. The solar power alternative usually comes as a slimline solar panel which includes a rechargeable battery that is connected to the panel. The panel shade or panel collects the energy from the sun throughout the day, making it an energy-efficient source that is suitable for homes with a lot of windows.


Advantages of Smart Locks 1. Enhanced Security: Enhanced Security: Smart locks often offer advanced security features such as PIN codes, biometric authentication, and real-time activity monitoring, reducing the risk of unauthorized access.
2. Convenience: Keyless entry allows for remote control, enabling homeowners to grant access to guests or service providers even when they're not at home.
3. Activity Monitoring: Many smart locks maintain detailed activity logs, helping users keep tabs on who enters and exits their property, enhancing security and providing peace of mind.
4. Temporary Access: Temporary Access: Smart locks allow homeowners to provide temporary access codes, reducing the need for physical keys and minimizing the risk of key loss.

Disadvantages of Smart Locks 1. Power Dependency: Smart locks often rely on batteries, and if they run out or lose power, users may be locked out unless they have a backup key or power source. Although, there are available motorized keyless-entrys that have backup batteries or solar energy converters in case a power outage occurs. But still becomes an inconvenience for areas that have frequent power outages, and with technology, we know that the possibility of glitches can still occur.
2. Compatibility Issues: Integrating smart locks with different devices and platforms can lead to compatibility challenges, necessitating careful consideration of your specific ecosystem.
3. Cost: Smart locks can be more expensive than traditional locks, and ongoing costs may be associated with maintenance and upgrades, potentially impacting your budget.
4. Vulnerable to hacking: If not adequately secured, smart locks can be vulnerable to hacking attempts, emphasizing the importance of strong passwords and regular software updates.

Costs of Smart Locks As of October 2021, motorized keyless-entrys cost between $350 to $700 per window (depending upon size). If you plan to make it a DIY project, you’ll save around $35 to $75 per window as there are motorized keyless-entry kits that offer easy-to-follow instructions for homeowners.
To provide a estimate for you, a 120cm x 90cm standard window will cost you around $220 to $350. The more windows you have, the more savings you get for a DIY installation. Materials:

Installation Cost: Using our 120cm x 90cm size window as a basis, you’ll spend around $500. Add 3% per additional square metre to your cost. So, for instance, a 1.5 square metre window will cost you $560 for your installation cost.
Factors to Consider: Aside from the window size, material choice, motor, and modifications, geographical location can also influence your motorized keyless-entry cost.


Is Keyless Entry Worth It?

Overall, even with all the pros and cons of motorized keyless-entrys, they’re still a worthy investment for homeowners who seek convenience, safety, and aesthetic appeal and can afford the upfront cost of installing smart keyless-entrys. Quality-made electric keyless-entrys can also last a lifetime and provides presets that automatically adjust your keyless-entrys for efficiency in your lighting and ventilation, ensuring a comfortable interior. Motorized keyless-entrys can be an ultimate energy-saving system for homes with many windows that have large or hard-to-reach windows.
